## Case Study: UPI "Collect Request" Fraud
# Category: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

# What the Crime Is
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves deceiving a victim into authorizing a transaction that drains their bank account. The most common variant is the "Request Money" scam, where the attacker reverses the logic of a standard payment to trick the user into "receiving" money that is actually being deducted.

# How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

The Hook: The attacker finds a victim on a secondary marketplace (like OLX or Facebook Marketplace) posing as a buyer for an item the victim is selling.

The Trust Move: The attacker agrees to the price immediately to avoid suspicion and claims they will pay via UPI.

The Deception: Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a "Collect Request" through a UPI app (like GPay or PhonePe). They tell the victim, "I have sent the payment; you just need to tap 'Pay' and enter your PIN to authorize the receipt."

The Theft: The moment the victim enters their UPI PIN, they authorize a debit from their own account to the attacker’s account.

The Exit: The attacker immediately disconnects the call and deactivates their account or moves the funds through multiple "mule" accounts.

# Who is Usually Targeted
Individuals selling items online, small business owners unfamiliar with the technical nuances of UPI, and senior citizens are the primary targets.

# Consequences
Victims face immediate financial loss, which is often difficult to reverse because the transaction was "authorized" via a PIN. Beyond the monetary hit, it leads to a loss of trust in digital payment ecosystems and significant psychological stress.
